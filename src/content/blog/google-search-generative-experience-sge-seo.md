## **A New Frontier in Search: Google's Generative AI**

Google is no stranger to leveraging AI for improving its search experience. For many years, AI has quietly played a pivotal role in enhancing the quality, relevance, and dynamism of Google Search. The journey began as early as 2001 with the integration of an early spelling correction system that helped users swiftly find relevant results, even if their queries contained typos or misspellings.

Fast forward to 2019, Google introduced the _Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers_ (BERT) to Search ranking, marking a significant milestone. Unlike conventional models that analyzed words in isolation, BERT considered the context surrounding each word. This leap in technology enabled users to pose longer, conversational-style queries while still receiving highly relevant search results.

The progress didn't stop at BERT. Google later adopted even more advanced large language models, specifically the _Multitask Unified Model_ (MUM). MUM, with its robust capabilities, is astonishingly 1,000 times more potent than BERT. This powerhouse model, trained in 75 languages, has been the backbone of various features in Google Search, pushing the boundaries of what's possible in the search engine landscape.

For a deeper dive into Google's advancements and vision in the world of generative AI, [check out this comprehensive overview](https://static.googleusercontent.com/media/www.google.com/en//search/howsearchworks/google-about-SGE.pdf) penned by [Srinivasan Venkatachary](https://www.linkedin.com/in/srinivasanvenkatachary/) and collaborators. This document offers a thorough understanding of the Search Generative Experience (SGE) and sheds light on how Google envisions the future of search with generative AI at its helm.

## **Understanding Google SGE and Generative AI in SEO**

At its core, the _Google Search Generative Experience_, often simply termed as SGE, is a sophisticated system wherein AI-powered algorithms work diligently behind the scenes to generate content that is most relevant to the user's query. This AI-generated content is designed to enhance organic search results by providing users with answers that are tailored to their specific needs.

For content creators, this means a shift in focus. It's no longer solely about optimizing content for search engines; it's about creating content that aligns with Google's SGE and other similar AI systems. The focus must be on high-quality, relevant, and informative content. With SGE, the aim is to make every click in the search results pages count.

## Google SGE Frequently Asked Questions

****What's this Google SGE I keep hearing about?****

Google SGE, or Search Generative Experience, is Google's new AI-driven system that generates content to enhance organic search results based on a user's query. Think of it as Google's AI providing more tailored answers to your questions.

****Does Google search really use generative AI?****

Absolutely! Google has been integrating AI into its search for years, with the latest being the Search Generative Experience.

****Wait, so Google can generate content on the fly?****

Not quite, not yet at least. Unlike ChatGPT, Google SGE will be a new way to search Google's search engine. Google has an AI Chatbot named Bard. It's worth noting Bard is built for research, whereas ChatGPT is the better content writer.

**I've heard of BERT before. How's SGE different?**

While BERT was all about understanding the context of words in search queries, Google's newer models like MUM and SGE take it up a notch. They're more about understanding and generating content that aligns closely with user intent.

**What is Google's generative AI examples?**

One example is how Google search now understands and responds to longer, conversational-style queries by using tech like BERT and MUM. SGE takes it further by generating relevant content for search results.

**I'm in the content game. How does this affect my SEO strategy?**

It's all about shifting focus. Content should be of high quality, informative, and in tune with natural language queries. It's more about serving the user now than just pleasing the search engine.

**How do I get Google search generative experience?**

As a user, you'll automatically see it in action when you use Google Search. As a content creator, focus on quality content, and you'll naturally align with Google's SGE.

**Okay, and how do I access Google SGE?**

1) Click on Google Search Labs Icon on Google Chrome.
2) Enable the SGE Button.
3) Click I Agree in the pop-up.
4) Click on Try an Example.
You can access SGE in the latest version of the Google app (Android or iOS) or on a Chrome desktop/browser.
_Not all accounts are eligible to access Search Labs._

**Which country has Google SGE?**

Google's SGE is currently only available in the US (English).

**So, does this mean keyword stuffing is totally out?**

Ralph Marino, our SEO expert, would say: "It's about genuine content for the user." Keyword stuffing hasn't worked for a while now & definitely won't fly in this AI-driven world. Focus on genuine, user-centric content!
