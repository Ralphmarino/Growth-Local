Search didn't disappear — it changed shape. A growing share of high-intent
questions now get answered inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI
Overviews before anyone clicks a result. That shift has a name: **GEO**
(Generative Engine Optimization) and its cousin **AEO** (Answer Engine
Optimization).

## Why this matters now

When an AI model answers a question, it cites a handful of sources. Being one of
those sources is the new page-one. If you're invisible to the models, you're
invisible to a fast-growing slice of your market — regardless of how well you
rank in classic search.

## What actually moves the needle

- **Be unambiguously citable.** Clear, factual, well-structured content that a
  model can lift and attribute without hedging.
- **Strengthen your entity.** Consistent naming, structured data, and authority
  signals help models understand *who you are* and *what you're credible on*.
- **Answer the real question.** AEO rewards content built around genuine intent,
  not keyword density.

## The good news

GEO and AEO aren't a separate discipline bolted onto SEO — they're the natural
extension of doing technical, on-page, and authority work well. The brands that
invested in genuine quality are the ones the models are already quoting.

> The fundamentals didn't change. The surface they pay off on did.

If you want to know where you stand with AI search today, that's exactly what a
modern SEO audit should tell you.
